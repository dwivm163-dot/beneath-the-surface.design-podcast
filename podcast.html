ABOUT THIS EPISODE

How one designer is using craft-based luxury and retail reform to challenge who benefits from fashion. We explore how Aurora James's work with African artisans and her Fifteen Percent Pledge are reshaping supply chains, representation, and economic justice in the industry.

Through Brother Vellies, James builds small-batch, craft-centered products that sustain artisan livelihoods and material ethics‚Äîthe polar opposite of fast-fashion practices. Her non-profit, the Fifteen Percent Pledge, helps shift power structures by pushing to actively re-route value (craft knowledge, jobs, shelf space, capital) to communities typically excluded by fashion and big retail.
