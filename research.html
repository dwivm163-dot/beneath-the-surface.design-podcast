EP. 01 Aurora James: Design as Activism

ABOUT AURORA JAMES

Toronto-native and New York City-transplant, Aurora James is the Creative Director and Founder of luxury accessories brand Brother Vellies. Founded in 2013 with the goal of keeping traditional African design practices and techniques alive while creating and sustaining artisanal jobs, Brother Vellies is now handmade across the globe.

Prior to founding Brother Vellies, James amassed an impressive resume of industry experience. Her background in fashion, journalism, art, photography, and horticulture joins a passion for artisanship, design, and humanitarianism to create one-of-a-kind pieces. Brother Vellies is worn regularly by Beyoncé, Meghan Markle, Zendaya, Rihanna, Serena Williams, Lady Gaga, Katy Perry, and Solange.

INITIATIVE

The Fifteen Percent Pledge

In June 2020, Aurora founded The Fifteen Percent Pledge, an initiative urging retail giants to commit 15% of their shelf space to Black-owned businesses. Within the first year, over 2 dozen major corporations dedicated to the Pledge, effectively diverting over $5B in capital to Black entrepreneurs in the United States.

BRAND

Brother Vellies

A craft-based luxury brand that builds small-batch, craft-centered products sustaining artisan livelihoods and material ethics—the polar opposite of fast-fashion practices. James works with artisans across Africa, giving them agency to produce entire products rather than just components.

APPROACH

Design Beyond the Object

Being a designer means seeing beyond the actual object and its function to the people, places, and interactions it impacts. James uses craft-based luxury and retail reform to highlight the hidden impacts of design choices on craft ecologies, supply chains, capital access, and representation.

IMPACT

Systemic Change

The Fifteen Percent Pledge helps shift power structures by pushing to actively re-route value—craft knowledge, jobs, shelf space, capital—to communities typically excluded by fashion and big retail, particularly Black-owned businesses.

KEY FINDINGS: FIFTEEN PERCENT PLEDGE IMPACT

  • 625+ Black-owned businesses have developed relationships with companies that have taken the Pledge
  • $14B Potential capital shift to Black entrepreneurs and businesses through the Pledge's work
  • 29 Companies across three countries have committed to multi-year contractual agreements, some as long as 10 years
