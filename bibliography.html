BIBLIOGRAPHY

Annotated bibliography of books, papers, and articles cited across our research on Aurora James, Brother Vellies, and the Fifteen Percent Pledge.

EP. 01 Aurora James: Design as Activism
BOOK

Design Justice: Community-Led Practices to Build the Worlds We Need

Sasha Costanza-Chock

Cambridge: MIT Press, 2020

Provides a structure for extending creative agency to groups disproportionately affected by inequality, integrating guidelines, practical case studies, and bottom-up approaches. Useful for expanding on the hidden, collateral implications of design choices—who gains, who loses, and how infrastructures might be reshaped outside of just the final product. Published by a respected academic press and widely cited across design studies.

GUIDELINES

Guidelines for Social Life Cycle Assessment of Products and Organizations 2020

United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) Life Cycle Initiative

Paris: UNEP, 2020

Provides a standardized procedure of assessment for the cultural and socioeconomic implications throughout a product or corporation's life cycle, including employees, community members, customers, and industry players. Offers an efficient framework for mapping and evaluating underlying implications like living standards and preservation of craftsmanship that standard environmental LCA may overlook. Developed under a United Nations–backed initiative with expert contributors.

ARTICLE

Retailers' Shelves Are Changing Thanks to Aurora James

Cady Lang

TIME, February 1, 2024

Gives a concise picture of what the Fifteen Percent Pledge has achieved—who signed on, what changed on shelves, and why it matters. Useful as a current snapshot to ground discussion of impacts that go beyond the shoe itself, and points toward follow-up questions about how those numbers are measured.

PRIMARY SOURCE

Brother Vellies Official Website

Brother Vellies

brothervellies.com

The brand's website outlines mission, product lines, materials, and artisan partnerships, offering detail on small-batch production, craft techniques, and pricing. Useful for tracing a product life cycle in concrete terms and documenting stated intentions around craft preservation and ethical production. As the company's own communication, it's informative but inherently promotional.

ARTICLE

Aurora James on Her 15 Percent Pledge Campaign to Support Small Business

Vogue

Vogue, 2020

Documents the early development of the 15 Percent Pledge and explores how Aurora James uses her platform to hold corporations accountable for inclusivity. Emphasizes the personal and strategic origins of the campaign, showing how social media activism evolved into a national movement. Reveals the hidden political and structural dimensions of design as social intervention.

ARTICLE

Brother Vellies Makes Shoes With a Conscience

Business of Fashion

Business of Fashion, 2019

Focuses on Brother Vellies' commitment to sustainability and craftsmanship. Details James's collaborations with African artisans and her use of ethically sourced materials. Highlights how local economies benefit from her design choices while raising awareness about global sustainability practices.

ARTICLE

Aurora James Addresses DEI Rollbacks, Consumer Boycotts

Black Enterprise

Black Enterprise, 2024

Covers Aurora James's reflections on the challenges of maintaining diversity and equity initiatives amid changing political and corporate climates. Highlights how her work has faced both backlash and systemic resistance. Provides real-world examples of the unforeseen consequences of socially motivated design, contributing a critical perspective on the "hidden" outcomes of activism within the design industry.

PRIMARY SOURCE

Aurora James – Fifteen Percent Pledge

Fifteen Percent Pledge

15percentpledge.org

Official source outlining Aurora James's founding of the 15 Percent Pledge. Provides foundation for understanding the organizational structure, partnerships, and measurable impacts of the pledge. Establishes how James's design activism extends into systemic retail reform.

ARTICLE

Activist & Fashion Designer Aurora James Shares Trial, Triumph, and Struggle With Identity in New Memoir

Jeroslyn JoVonn

Black Enterprise, May 11, 2023

Discusses designer Aurora James's memoir "Wildflower," summarizing James' book and showcasing the designer's personal narrative to confront themes of identity, activism, and adversity. Explores James' key life events, cultural background, challenges in education, and the 15% pledge calling upon racial and economic justice. Focuses on how James connects with her "mixed-race" identity and how it affected her public success.

ARTICLE

How Fifteen Percent Pledge Supports Black-Owned Businesses

Zoe Milan

NBC News, March 8, 2023

Outlines the overall mission and progress of the 15% Pledge. Emphasizes the tangible impact on Black business owners—giving opportunities through large retail spaces such as Sephora, Nordstrom, and Macy's, increasing visibility, customers, and revenue. Explains how James' decision of 15% was based on the population consensus in the United States.

RESEARCH

Study: Black Entrepreneurship in the United States

Tiffany Howard (UNLV Political Science)

UNLV News Center, July 24, 2020

Focuses on the current state of Black-owned businesses in the United States. Analyzes the consistent lag in Black entrepreneurs, citing barriers such as the racial wealth gap, restricted capital, fewer mentorship opportunities, and structural inequalities within institutions. Offers potential solutions including investments, support in public policies, and program initiatives to build entrepreneurial skills.

ARTICLE

Fewer Black Men Are Enrolling in HBCUs. Here's Why and What's Being Done

Juana Summers, Jason Fuller, Patrick Jarenwattananon

NPR, October 28, 2024

Examines the significant decline in Black male enrollment at Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs)—now only 26% compared to 38% in 1976. Discusses factors including financial barriers, inadequate public school education, and lack of Black male educators. Spotlights broader implications for HBCUs and the communities they serve.

ARTICLE

Aurora James on Fashion, Feminism, and the Fifteen Percent Pledge

Janelle Okwodu

Vogue, August 26, 2020

Aurora James founded Brother Vellies, a Black-owned and female-led lifestyle and accessories brand redefining luxury with a mission to uplift traditional artisans and sustainable practices. Community is essential to her brand and identity. Her work, especially the Fifteen Percent Pledge, is about authenticity and human connection.

ARTICLE

As the 15 Percent Pledge Marks Its 3-Year Anniversary, Aurora James Asserts 'We're Just Getting Started'

Karin Eldor

Forbes, June 20, 2023

Reflection upon the 3rd anniversary of the 15 Percent Pledge. Key finding: More than 625 Black-owned businesses have developed business relationships with companies that have taken the Pledge, creating the potential to shift over $14 billion to Black entrepreneurs. To date, 29 companies across three countries have committed to multi-year contractual agreements, some as long as 10 years.

ARTICLE

Brother Vellies' Aurora James Merges High Fashion With Global Artisans

Lindsay Schneider

Observer, August 2, 2016

Highlights Aurora James's consistent use of artisans within Africa—not relying on them for just one component but the whole shoe, giving them further agency to produce on their own. She makes direct efforts to not only build community with artisans but provide real things they might need like a rainwater purifier. James rejects norms set by the industry and wants to be pro-employees.

ARTICLE

Aurora James on DEI Rollbacks, Boycotts and the Future of Black-Owned Brands

Fashionista

Fashionista, February 7, 2025

James argues for DEI as a strength—not just an initiative but as a way to have diverse perspectives which could open doors that previously wouldn't be known. Her consistent effort to empower Black designers within her own company and beyond. She holds companies accountable for rolling back DEI programs and not respecting their workers.
